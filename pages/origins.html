Letters from the Trenches

The War Begins (1914-1918)

In the summer of 1914, Europe was plunged into a devastating conflict known as the First World War. More than 30 nations would become involved, and millions of lives were forever changed.

Life in the Trenches

Soldiers on the front lines lived in narrow trenches dug into the ground. The conditions were cold, muddy, and filled with danger. Letters from home were treasured, and writing back was a way to cope with the fear and loneliness.

The Western Front

Most of the fighting occurred in Belgium and northern France. Armies built long trench lines across the countryside, and battles were fought for months over just a few kilometers of land.
