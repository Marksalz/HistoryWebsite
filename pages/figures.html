Private Thomas Whitmore

Thomas Whitmore was a 19-year-old British soldier who enlisted in 1915, full of patriotism and fear. He left behind his family farm in Yorkshire to fight in the Great War. Through his letters, we follow his personal thoughts, fears, and moments of hope during the darkest days of trench warfare.

Letter Excerpt - March 1915

"We move at first light tomorrow. Some say this push will end it all, but I'm not so sure. I miss the smell of Mum's bread more than I thought possible."

A Life Interrupted

Like many young men, Thomas dreamed of returning home and starting a family. He never made it back. He died during the Battle of the Somme in 1916, one of the deadliest battles of the war. His final letter was never sent, but it was found folded in his coat pocket.
