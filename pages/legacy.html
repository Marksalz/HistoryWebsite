The Legacy of Thomas Whitmore

Though Thomas Whitmore never returned home, his story lives on — through letters, family memories, and the generations who continue to honor those who served. His fictional experience reflects the real sacrifice of millions whose names may never be known.

Remembrance Day

Every year on November 11th, people across the world observe a moment of silence for those lost in war. In the UK and Commonwealth, red poppies are worn to remember the fallen — a tradition that began after WWI.

Letters That Taught Us

War letters are now studied in classrooms, museums, and libraries. They teach us about fear, friendship, and the human spirit during times of chaos. Through fictional letters like Thomas’s, we preserve that voice.

Why We Remember

Remembering those who served is not about glorifying war — it is about understanding its cost. The stories of soldiers like Thomas help remind us of our shared humanity, and our responsibility to build peace.
